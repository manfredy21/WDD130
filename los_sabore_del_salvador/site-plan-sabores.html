And we want you to know our food, the next information will help you The pupusa is a thick corn or rice dough-based tortilla filled with one or more ingredients, such as cheese, pork rinds, squash, refried beans, loroco, etc. Due to its tradition, versatility and low cost, it is one of the most widespread foods in El Salvador and Honduras, countries that dispute their patrimonial origin. Although its origin is unknown for certain, it is most likely a mestizo dish, between native Mesoamerican cuisine and the Spanish culinary tradition that was imported during colonization and subsequent migrations. Several recent scholars affirm that the pupusa has a Quiché Mayan origin. Tamales are a dish that is part of the typical food of El Salvador and it is believed that they were prepared before the Spanish conquest. In addition, they were considered as a sacred food, typical of the gods. This food can be prepared with different ingredients, flavors and recipes. The preparation, dough, filling, wrapper, as well as the shape of the tamale can vary a bit depending on the country or region. Salvadoran pastelitos, also called salvadoran empanadas, are a type of stuffed, fried, succulent and crunchy empanadas that have become one of the most popular and simple fast foods in the country. Pastelitos are nothing more than small pies or cakes. They are traditional from several Latin American countries. In Costa Rica, Puerto Rico, the Dominican Republic, Trinidad and Tobago, the Caribbean, the Caribbean coast of Colombia, Uruguay, and Panama, they resemble tamales. In Hawaii, they are called patties. The cakes can be sweet or savory. Pastel is a Spanish and Portuguese word and is the name of different typical dishes from many countries that speak these languages. We challenge you to try our tasty food.

El Salvador?

The civilization of El Salvador dates back to pre-Columbian times, around 1500 BC, as evidenced by the ruins of Tazumal and Chalchuapa. The first inhabitants were the Pocomames, Lencas, and Pipiles, who settled in the central and western areas of the country in the mid-11th century. Spanish Admiral Andrés Niño led an expedition to Central America and landed on Isla Meanguera, located in the Gulf of Fonseca, on May 31, 1522. This was the first Salvadoran territory visited by the Spanish. In June 1524 the Spanish Captain Pedro de Alvarado began a war of conquest against Cuzcatlán (land of precious things) which was populated by the country's native tribes. After 17 days of bloody battles, many natives and Spaniards died, including the indigenous chief Atlacatl. Pedro de Alvarado defeated and wounded in his left hip, gave up the fight and retired to Guatemala, commissioning his brother, Gonzalo de Alvarado, to continue with the conquest of Cuzcatlán. Later, his cousin Diego de Alvarado established the town of San Salvador in April 1525, in a place called La Bermuda, near the city of Suchitoto. In 1546, Carlos I of Spain granted San Salvador the title of city. During the following years, the country evolved under Spanish rule. Towards the end of the year 1810, a feeling of freedom arose among the peoples of Central America and the moment to break the chains of slavery arrived at dawn on November 5, 1811, when the priest José Matías Delgado, replied the bells of the La Merced Church. in San Salvador, calling for an insurrection. After many internal struggles, the Central American Independence Act was signed in Guatemala on September 15, 1821. This day we commemorate the Independence Day of our land of Cuzcatleca. Important dates 1958 El Salvador and Guatemala invite Honduras, Nicaragua and Costa Rica to decide on the integration of a Constituent Assembly within the projected Central American Federation (August 29). 1969 Due to the expulsion of more than 11,000 Salvadorans residing in Honduras, an undeclared war broke out between the two countries. Salvadoran forces invade Honduran territory. (July 14). An emergency meeting of the OAS approves the Peace agreement between El Salvador and Honduras. (July 30). 1980 Three of the leftist organizations come together to coordinate the fight against the government. (January 10). The Junta announces a radical plan for Agrarian Reform. (6th of March). The Salvadoran Private Bank is nationalized. (7 of March). The Archbishop of San Salvador Oscar Arnulfo Romero is assassinated while officiating a mass. (March 24th). José Napoleón Duarte is named Head of State (December 13). 1981 José Napoleón Duarte announces the formation of a Constituent Assembly in 1982. (March 5). A new political party called Alianza Republicana Nacionalista (ARENA) is legally formed. (September 30th) 1982 Elections are held for the Constituent Assembly, Dr. Alvaro Magaña is designated as Provisional President. (28th March); who forms his cabinet with representatives of different political institutes. 1984 Elections are held for President of the Republic and José Napoleón Duarte is elected (July 1). 1985 Elections for Mayors and Deputies are held, with the Christian Democratic Party obtaining a relative majority and ARENA standing out as the second political force. 1988 Elections for Mayors and Deputies are held again and this time ARENA obtains the majority of Deputies in the Legislative Assembly, in addition to 178 Mayors out of the 262 that exist in the country. Along with this, they obtained by a large majority the Mayor's Office of San Salvador, which had been in the hands of the Christian Democrats for more than 20 years. 1989 On March 19, elections for President were held and Lic. Alfredo Félix Cristiani, candidate for the ARENA Party, was elected by 54.7% of the electorate. On November 11, the FMLN carried out an offensive which did not achieve the desired ends. 1992 On January 16, 1992, the Peace Accords were signed in Chapultepec.
